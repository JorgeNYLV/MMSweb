Tecate Overview

The city of Tecate is located in the center of the State of Baja California between Tijuana and Mexicali. It is easily accessible from Tecate, California and major U.S. and Mexico border cities in the region.

Getting There

From Los Angeles, I-5 South until you reach San Diego County, then take I-94 East for 35 miles, exit to Highway 188 South and follow the signs to Tecate. From San Diego take I-94E for 35 miles, then go south on Highway 188.

From Tijuana, it takes approximately 30 minutes via the East Toll Road.

From Mexicali City, go west on Federal Highway #2. The trip is approximately 1 hour 15 min.

Key Facts

Tecate’s public services include:

- Water

- Electricity

- Drainage and Sewage system

- Commercial, Digital & Cellular Telephony, etc.

- Natural and Liquefied Petroleum (LP) Gas

The city also has a strong business and logistics infrastructure which includes branches of major banks, a variety of freight carriers, rail access and customs brokers.

There are approximately 90,000 residents distributed in the Urban Zone and five Political Delegations in the rural zone. Approximately 80% of the population lives in the city.

Tecate has a strong industrial sector. In addition to two large beverage companies, there are 135 assembly plants, producing electronics, furniture and plastic ware. Companies in the area include:

- Schlage de México, SA

- Rockwell Automation

- Formula Plastics

- Quixote Corporation

- Hudson

- Taylor Guitars

- Prositec, SA

- Best RV

- Innovative Industries

Tecate’s climate and quality of life also make it a tourist region. There are a number of spas and rest centers in the area.

Industrial Zones and Parks

The City of Tecate has several industrial parks located in strategic development zones. These include: Tecate Industrial Park, Fundadores Industrial Park, Tecate Gateway and El Bajio Industrial Park.
